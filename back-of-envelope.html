🧮 Back-of-the-Envelope Estimation

Estimate QPS, storage, bandwidth, and memory fast and out loud under interview pressure. Precision doesn't matter — landing within ~2–3× with clean reasoning does. Round aggressively, keep the math in your head.
QPSstoragebandwidthmemorypeak factor
In the Systems Architecture round you must produce capacity numbers quickly and defend the magnitude. Interviewers care that you state assumptions, reason cleanly, and sanity-check powers of ten — not that you're exact.

Method — 5 steps

  1. Clarify scale assumptions. Ask for / state DAU, read:write ratio, object size, retention. Write them down — they drive everything.
  2. Average, then peak. Compute average QPS from daily volume, then apply a peak factor (peak ≈ 2× average default; bursty products 3–5×).
  3. Storage = objects × size × retention. New objects/day × bytes/object × days kept. Add replication (×3 common) if asked.
  4. Bandwidth = QPS × payload. Separate read vs write; reads usually dominate.
  5. Sanity-check magnitudes. GB vs PB? hundreds vs millions of QPS? Re-check powers of ten before committing.

Time shortcuts

  • Seconds in a day ≈ 86,400 ≈ 100,000 (105) — the single most useful shortcut.
  • Seconds in a month ≈ 2.5 million (2.5 × 106); in a year ≈ 31.5 million (~3 × 107).
  • 5 years ≈ 1,825 ≈ ~2,000 days.

QPS from DAU — rule of thumb

# 1. Average QPS
avg_QPS = (DAU × actions_per_user_per_day) / 86,400
        ≈ (DAU × actions) / 100,000        # 10^5 shortcut
# 2. Peak QPS
peak_QPS ≈ 2 × avg_QPS                      # 3–5× for bursty/event-driven
# 3. Split reads vs writes
apply read:write ratio (often 10:1 … 100:1)
Memorize the 105 shortcut: divide daily volume by 100,000 to get average QPS in one step.

Worked example — URL shortener (end to end)

Assumptions (state first): 100 M DAU × 1 new URL/day → 100 M writes/day · read:write 100:1 → 10 B reads/day · object ~500 B · retention 5 years.

1 · Write QPS
100 M / 86,400 ≈ 100 M / 10^5 = ~1,000 writes/s avg
peak ≈ 2× → ~2,000 writes/s
2 · Read QPS
10 B / 10^5 = ~100,000 reads/s avg
peak ≈ 2× → ~200,000 reads/s
3 · Storage (5 years)
100 M/day × 365 ≈ 36.5 B/year → 5 yr ≈ ~180 B objects
× 500 B = 9 × 10^13 B = ~90 TB  (×3 replication → ~270 TB)
4 · Bandwidth
write: 2,000 × 500 B = 1 MB/s        # negligible
read: 200,000 × 500 B = 100 MB/s = ~0.8 Gbps peak
5 · Cache memory (80/20)
cache hot 20% of distinct daily objects:
20 M × 500 B = ~10 GB           # a few cache nodes
Sanity check: ~1k writes/s, ~100k reads/s — a read-heavy mid-large service; ~90 TB over 5 years; sub-Gbps bandwidth. All believable magnitudes. ✅

Practice drills

~3–5 min each, out loud, assumptions first. Work the magnitude yourself, then reveal. Targets land within ~2–3×.

🔗 URL shortener — write QPS, read QPS, 5-yr storage (100 M DAU)
See the worked example above: ~1k write QPS (peak ~2k), ~100k read QPS (peak ~200k), ~90 TB over 5 years (×3 ≈ 270 TB).
🐦 Twitter timeline — home-timeline read QPS + 5-yr tweet storage
300 M DAU × a few timeline loads/day ÷ 105 ≈ low-hundreds-of-thousands read QPS at peak. Tweets: ~500 M/day × ~300 B ≈ ~150 GB/day → ~270 TB over 5 years (pre-replication). Reads dominate massively → cache + fan-out.
📷 Photo storage — write QPS + 5-yr storage (100 M DAU, 1 photo/day)
100 M writes/day → ~1,000 write QPS (peak ~2k). Storage: 100 M × ~500 KB × 365 × 5 ≈ ~90 PB. The headline is petabytes → object store, not a DB. Bandwidth dominated by reads/CDN.
🎬 Video streaming — egress bandwidth for 10 M concurrent HD streams
10 M × 5 Mbps = 50 Tbps egress. The number is enormous → CDN edge caching is the entire design point, not origin bandwidth.
💬 Chat — message QPS + daily storage (500 M DAU × 40 msg/day)
500 M × 40 = 20 B msg/day ÷ 105 = ~200k write QPS avg, peak ~400k–1M. Storage: 20 B × 1 KB = 20 TB/day → retention drives it (90 days ≈ ~1.8 PB).
🚦 Rate limiter — RAM to track per-user counts for 100 M active users
100 M × ~16–50 B/counter (user id + count + window) ≈ 2–5 GB. Fits in memory on a few Redis nodes, sharded by user.
📣 News-feed fan-out — write amplification when a 10 M-follower user posts
Celebrity post = 10 M timeline writes per post (fan-out-on-write). This is the classic argument for hybrid: fan-out-on-write for normal users, fan-out-on-read (pull) for high-follower accounts.
🔍 Search index — index storage for 10 B docs × 10 KB each
10 B × 10 KB = 100 TB raw. The inverted index is a fraction of raw text, but postings + positions can rival it → estimate tens to hundreds of TB with replication. Sharded across many machines.
🔔 Notifications — peak QPS to push to 50 M users within 5 minutes
50 M ÷ 300 s ≈ ~167k QPS sustained. Needs a queue + many workers; provision peak above sustained.
📊 Analytics counter — event QPS + 1-yr storage for 1 B page views/day
1 B ÷ 105 = ~10k events/s avg, peak ~20–50k. Raw storage: 1 B × ~100 B = 100 GB/day → aggregate / roll up rather than store raw long-term.
